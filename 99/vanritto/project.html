THE PROJECT

OnTheMoveFundraising

Developing an Engaging Web Solution for The Liberty Project's Bike Fundraiser

The project was developed by the team as the team is currently working with the non-profit, The Liberty Project to develop a webpage on their website dedicated to their new fundraising initiative, a multi-day bike fundraising event.

The Problem Statement

What is the ideal outcome for this project?

To have a way for people to be able view where the riders are on the course for a multi-day bike fundraising event to increase engagement for the event as is it occurring.

What is the current reality?

There is no way for users to see where the riders are on the event course for the fundraiser event while it is occurring. In addition, there is limited funding from the organization to build a solution to solve the current problem.

What are the consequences if a solution is not created?

If users cannot view the riders on the course during the event, there may be loss interest while event is occurring which may result in an overall loss of day-of donations and engagement from home. This is a limited time event so the more excitement that is generated on the days that the event is occurring will potentially able lead to more donations and as well as knowledge of the organization itself to the general public.

What is the teams proposal?

The team will generate a web map that will showcase where the riders are on the course for the multi-day bike fundraiser event, OnTheMoveFundraising, for The Liberty Project.

The Journey to the Solution

The Beginning

The original idea that was generated to solve the Problem Statement was to use Live Tracking where the riderâ€™s live location would be shown on the webpage created for the fundraising event and users could see how the rides are progressing on the route during the day and over the course of the multi-day event. After looking at options to achieve Live Tracking and consulting with the advisor for the project, it was found that integrating live tracking onto a webpage would need a designated App which is outside the scope of this particular project and for the product that is going to be built for The Liberty Project. Upon this information, the team set off to find alternative solutions to solve the Problem Statement.

The Middle Steps

While exploring alternatives to Live Tracking that would still carry the essence of visually showing where the riders were on the designated course to increase public engagement and interest of the event, the team generated multiple solutions that could be used for the event's webpage through Mapbox functionalities as well as two other notable discoveries - one using Mapbox and another through widgets
