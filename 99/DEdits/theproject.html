    The Project

    OnTheMoveFundraising

    Developing an Engaging Web Solution for The Liberty Project's Bike Fundrasier

    The project was developed by the team as the team is currently working with the non-profit, The Liberty Project to develop a webpage on their website dedicated to their new fundraising initiave, a multi-day bike fundraising even

    The Problem Statement

    What is the ideal outcome from this project?

    TO have a way for people to be able view where the riders are on the course for a multi-day bike fundraising event to increase engagment for the event.

    What is the current reality?

    There is no way for viewers to see where the riders are on the event course for the fundraiser event while it is occurring. In addition, there is limited funding from the organization.

    What are the consequences if a solution is not created?

    If supporters/viewers cannot view the riders on the course during the event, there may be loss interest on day(s) of event which may result in overall loss of day-of donations and engagment from home (COVID, etc). This is a limited time event so the more excitement that is built on the day of will potentially able to generate more donations and knowledge of the organization to the general public.

    What is our group's proposal?

    The team will generate a web map that will showcase where the riders are on the course for the multi-day bike fundraiser event, OnTheMoveFundraising, for The Liberty Project.

    Technology To Be Explored and Used

    Mapbox

    Animated Time Segments

    Interactive Storytelling

    The original idea that was generated to solve the problem was to have live tracking of the riders during the event shown on the webpage generated for the fundraising event. After looking at numeerous options to acheive this and consulting with the advisor for the project, it was concluded that integrating live tracking onto a webpage would need a designated app and the team set off to find alternative solutions to solve the problem. While exploring alternatives to live tracking that would still carry the essence of visually showing the riders on the designated course and increasing engagement of the event for the public, the team generated multiple potential ideas that could be used for the event's webpage. The team explored three (or is it two?) solutions that could be generated to : 1. Animated Time Segments Using Mapbox 2. Interactive Storytelling Using Mapbox
